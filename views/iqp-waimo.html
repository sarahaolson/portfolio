Assessing the Impact of Sediment in Wairarapa Moana

Tools Used

Final Cut Pro X


A 35 minute documentary to explore a surrounding community’s changing connection with a polluted wetland in New Zealand to support efforts for change

ABOUT

At WPI, the Interactive Qualifying Project (IQP) “challenges students to address a problem that lies at the intersection of science or technology with society”. With over 50 project centers around the world, I had the opportunity to complete a project based out of Wellington, Aotearoa (New Zealand).

The goal of our project was to understand how the health of Wairarapa Moana, a large wetland in the North Island, impacts the connection between its surrounding community and environment. Originally our project was going to focus on the intersections between Western science and mātaurange Māori. However, shortly after we arrived in Aotearoa, our project partners expressed that they did not see the Māori perspective in our proposal. As a result, we decided to shift the focus toward the significance of the connection between Wairarapa Moana and its surrounding community, and explore how/if the sediment was changing it.
